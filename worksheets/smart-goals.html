SMART Goals

A structured framework for collaboratively setting goals that are Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound.

My SMART Goal Worksheet

To be completed together with a clinician.

Use this worksheet to turn a broad hope or intention into a clear, actionable goal. Work through each section with your clinician, then write your completed SMART goal at the bottom.

Starting Point: What Do You Want?
Before breaking it down, write out the broad goal or intention in your own words. Don't worry about being precise yet.
Examples: "I want to feel less anxious", "I want to get fit", "I want to be more connected to people."

Using your answers above, write your goal out as one clear statement that covers all five elements.
