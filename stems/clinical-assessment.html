Initial Assessment Interview

The initial clinical assessment aims to build rapport, gather a comprehensive picture of the client's presenting difficulties, and begin to form a working formulation. These stems support a structured yet flexible approach to assessment, covering the key domains recommended by Ivey, Ivey & Zalaquett (2018).

Assessment · Ivey, Ivey & Zalaquett (2018)
Opening the Session
Setting the frame, building rapport, and inviting the client to share their reason for attending
01What has brought you in today to see me?
02Can you tell me a bit about what's been going on for you recently?
03What would you most like help with?
04Is there anything you'd like to know about how this session will work before we begin?
05What made you decide to seek help now, at this point in time?

Exploring the Presenting Problem
Building a detailed picture of the main difficulty — onset, frequency, duration, and history
01When did you first notice this becoming a problem?
02Can you walk me through what happens when [the symptom or behaviour] occurs?
03How often does this happen, and how long does it typically last?
04What were things like before this started?
05Has anything like this happened before — at an earlier time in your life?
06On a typical day, can you describe what things look like from when you wake up to when you go to sleep?

Exploring Triggers
Identifying situations, contexts, and events that activate or worsen the presenting difficulty
01Are there particular situations or times when this tends to be worse?
02What was happening around the time these difficulties began?
03Is there anything that makes it better, even temporarily?
04Can you think of the most recent time it happened — what was going on just before?
05Are there certain people, places, or situations that tend to set it off?

Exploring Impact
Understanding how the difficulty affects daily life, relationships, and functioning
01How is this affecting your day-to-day life — work, relationships, daily activities?
02What have you had to change or give up because of this?
03How are the people around you responding to what's going on?
04Is this affecting your sleep, appetite, or energy levels?
05What would your life look like if this difficulty wasn't there?

Thoughts & Beliefs
Exploring the cognitive content — automatic thoughts, meanings, and underlying beliefs
01When that happens, what goes through your mind?
02What does that experience mean to you — what do you make of it?
03Is there a thought or belief that keeps coming back in those moments?
04What's the first thought that goes through your head when you wake up in the morning?
05What does that say about you — or about how you see yourself?

Emotions & Physical Sensations
Identifying emotional experiences and somatic symptoms that accompany the presenting difficulty
01What emotions come up for you in those situations?
02Do you notice any physical sensations alongside those feelings?
03How intense would you say those feelings are, on a scale of 0 to 10?
04Where in your body do you tend to feel it most?
05How long do those feelings usually last once they come on?

Coping & Safety Behaviours
Understanding the client's current strategies for managing distress — both helpful and maintaining
01What do you do when you start to feel that way?
02Have you found anything that helps, even short-term?
03Are there things you avoid because of this?
04Do you find yourself checking, seeking reassurance, or doing things in a particular way to manage the anxiety?
05What have you tried so far — and how has that worked out?

Risk & Safety
Sensitively assessing hopelessness, self-harm, suicidal ideation, and protective factors
01Have you had any thoughts of harming yourself or not wanting to be here?
02Do you feel hopeful about the future?
03Is there anyone you feel you can turn to for support?
04Sometimes when people are going through a difficult time they can have thoughts about ending their life — is that something you've experienced?
05What keeps you going, even on the hardest days?
