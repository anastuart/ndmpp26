Vicious Flower Formulation

Stems for exploring the six interacting cycles that maintain distress — automatic threat processing, safety behaviours, attention bias, reasoning biases, emotional responses, and memory bias.

The Six-Petal Model

The vicious flower visualises six interacting maintenance cycles as petals around a central core of distress. Each petal feeds into the others, creating a self-reinforcing loop. Formulation involves mapping the client's experience onto each petal and identifying which cycles are most active.

Automatic Threat Processing
Exploring the rapid, often unconscious activation of the threat system — perceptions of danger, rejection, or failure
01When you first notice yourself feeling distressed, what is the very first thing that goes through your mind?
02What kinds of situations tend to trigger that sense of threat or danger for you?
03How quickly does that feeling come on — does it seem automatic, or is it something you think your way into?
04Is it like your brain jumps straight to a sense of danger before you've even had time to think it through?
05What does your body do in those first moments — before you've consciously made sense of what's happening?

Safety Behaviours
Identifying actions aimed at reducing threat or avoiding harm — and how they prevent disconfirmation of fears
01When you feel that way, what do you do to try to feel safer or more comfortable?
02Are there things you avoid doing because of how they might make you feel?
03Do those strategies help in the moment? What about in the longer term?
04What do you think would happen if you didn't do [the safety behaviour]?
05Have you ever noticed that the more you do [the behaviour], the more you seem to need it?
06Does avoiding [the situation] give you a chance to find out whether your fear would actually come true?

Attention Bias
Exploring selective focus on threat-related cues — hypervigilance and narrowed attention
01When you're in that state, what do you tend to notice or focus on?
02Do you find yourself scanning for signs that things might go wrong?
03Are there positive things happening around you that you might be overlooking?
04If someone gave you a compliment and a criticism in the same conversation, which would stick with you more?
05Do you notice that you're particularly tuned in to other people's reactions or expressions?

Reasoning Biases
Identifying distorted thinking patterns that confirm threat beliefs — catastrophising, mind-reading, overgeneralising
01When you think about the situation, do you tend to jump to the worst-case scenario?
02Do you find yourself reading into what other people might be thinking about you?
03Is there another way of looking at the situation that you might be discounting?
04When something goes wrong, does it feel like evidence that things always go wrong for you?
05Are you drawing a conclusion about yourself as a whole person based on this one event?
06If a friend described this same situation, would you reach the same conclusion about them?

Emotional Responses
Exploring strong emotional experiences and how they feed back into attention, reasoning, and behaviour
01What emotions come up most strongly for you when this happens?
02Do those feelings ever seem to confirm the thoughts you're having — like "I feel anxious, so something must be wrong"?
03How do those emotions affect what you do next?
04When the emotion is really intense, does it feel like it will last forever — or that you can't cope with it?
05Do you ever use how you're feeling as evidence for what's true — as though feeling bad means things are bad?

Memory Bias
Exploring how threat-related memories are preferentially recalled and shape current interpretations
01When you're feeling this way, what memories tend to come to mind?
02Do you find it easier to recall times things went badly rather than times they went well?
03How do those past experiences shape how you see the current situation?
04Is there a particular memory that keeps coming back when you feel like this?
05If you tried to think of a time when things actually went well in a similar situation, how easy or hard would that be?
06Does this current situation remind you of something from your past — and could that be colouring how you see it now?
