Question Stems

Motivational Interviewing

Open, collaborative stems for guiding a client through their own ambivalence — eliciting change talk, exploring discrepancy, and supporting self-efficacy. The aim is to help the client harvest the seed that's already there.

Filter:
Engaging & Opening
Wide, low-pressure invitations that signal curiosity rather than agenda. Start here to let the client set the scene in their own words.
01Tell me a bit about your [drinking / use / behaviour] — what does it look like for you on a typical day?
02What's brought you in today — in your own words?
03How are you feeling about being here and talking about this?
04What would be most useful for us to spend our time on today?
05Where would you like to start?
06Help me understand what a good day looks like for you — and what a hard one looks like.
07What's important for me to understand about you before we go any further?

Exploring the Pros
The behaviour is meeting a need — name it. Understanding what's working keeps the locus of control with the client and sets the scene for a realistic change plan.
01What are the good things about [the behaviour] for you?
02What do you like about it?
03What does it do for you that nothing else seems to?
04What else is good about it? Anything else that comes to mind?
05What would you miss if it weren't part of your life?
06When does it help you most — what's going on for you in those moments?
07What need is it meeting for you, do you think?

Exploring the Less-Good
Let the client — not you — name the downsides. The shift from "the good things" to "the less good things" is where ambivalence starts to surface.
01What's not so good about it for you?
02What concerns you, if anything, about how things are at the moment?
03What else is not so good? Anything more?
04What's the cost of this — for you, for the people around you?
05If you imagine a year from now and nothing has changed — what does that look like?
06What's it taking from you that you'd like back?
07Is there anything about it you wish were different?

Reflections — Simple & Complex
Simple reflections slow the client down and show them they're heard. Complex reflections infer the meaning underneath — the feeling, the conflict, the value at stake.
01It sounds like [paraphrase what they've just said].
02So for you, [behaviour] is a way of [meeting need].
03It sounds like part of you wants [X], and part of you wants [Y].
04You feel stuck between [two pulls].
05There's something underneath this that really matters to you.
06It's been heavy to carry on your own.
07So far you've told me [A, B, C] — does that capture it, or is there something I'm missing?

Affirmations
Specific, evidence-based acknowledgement of strengths, efforts, and values. Not flattery — recognition of what's already there.
01It took courage to come in and talk about this.
02You've thought about this a lot — that comes through clearly.
03Even with everything going on, you've kept showing up.
04That's a real strength, the way you've [specific behaviour].
05You care about this — about [your kids / your work / your health] — and that's coming through in what you're saying.
06You've been honest with yourself about something a lot of people avoid.
07The fact you're even weighing this up tells me something about what matters to you.

Evoking Change Talk — Desire
Want, wish, hope. Questions that pull the client's own language about what they'd like to be different into the room.
01What do you want to be different about your [situation / drinking / relationship]?
02If things could be any way you wanted, what would they look like?
03What do you wish were different right now?
04What are you hoping for, in coming here?
05In an ideal world — no obstacles — how would this look for you?
06What kind of life do you want to be building?

Evoking Change Talk — Ability
Strengthen the client's belief that change is possible for them. Past successes, partial wins, and existing strengths all count.
01If you decided to make a change, what makes you think you could do it?
02What strengths do you have that might help you with this?
03When have you done something difficult before — what helped you then?
04Tell me about a time you went without [behaviour], even for a short while. How did you manage that?
05What's something about you that you think other people might underestimate?
06If you decided this was something you could do — what would that part of you sound like?

Evoking Change Talk — Reasons
The specific "becauses" that matter to this client. Avoid offering reasons — draw them out.
01What are the main reasons you would want this to be different?
02If you did make a change, what's the best thing that could come of it?
03How would things be better if you [stopped / cut back]?
04What worries you most about how things are now?
05What's the worst that could happen if nothing changes?
06Who else would notice if this got better — and what would they see?

Evoking Change Talk — Need
Urgency and importance — the felt sense that something has to give.
01How important is this for you to change, on a scale of 0 to 10?
02Why a [5] and not a [2]? What makes it that high?
03What would it take to move that number up by even one?
04How much does this need to change?
05What tells you something needs to be different?
06If you don't do anything about this, what happens?

Mobilising Change Talk — Commitment, Activation, Taking Steps
Once the "why" is loud, listen for and draw out the "I will". Even tiny steps count — change is the sum of small efforts repeated.
01What do you think you'll do?
02What are you ready to try?
03What feels like a step you could actually take this week?
04You said [change talk] — what would that look like in practice?
05What's something small you've already started doing?
06What's the very next step — even if it's tiny?
07When could you see yourself starting?

Developing Discrepancy
Hold up the gap between how things are and how the client wants them to be — gently, without arguing. Let them hear it themselves.
01On one hand you've said [A], and on the other hand [B]. How do you make sense of that?
02How does [behaviour] fit with the kind of [parent / partner / person] you want to be?
03What matters most to you in life — and how does this sit with that?
04If your [child / younger self] were watching, what would they make of this?
05Where do you want to be in five years — and where is this taking you?
06You mentioned this isn't who you want to be. Tell me about who you do want to be.

Rolling with Resistance
Resistance is a signal about the fit between your strategy and their readiness — not opposition to be overcome. Reflect it back, soften, and hand control back to the client.
01It sounds like that doesn't quite land for you — tell me more.
02You're the expert on your own life — I'm not here to tell you what to do.
03Part of you isn't sure this is even a problem.
04It makes sense you'd push back on that — anyone might.
05So as things stand, the costs of changing feel bigger than the costs of staying the same.
06Maybe this isn't the right time for you to be working on this — what would be useful?
07What would need to be true for this to feel worth changing?

Building Self-Efficacy
Importance gets people interested. Confidence gets them moving. Without belief in their ability, they won't act — even when they want to.
01How confident are you that you could do this, on a scale of 0 to 10?
02Why a [4] and not a [1]? What gives you that much?
03What would help that confidence number go up?
04What's worked for you in the past, when you've tried to change something?
05What got in the way last time — and what would you do differently now?
06Who in your life believes you can do this?
07If you didn't have to rely on willpower alone, what else might help?

Planning & Commitment
Help the client build the plan — don't hand it to them. Specific, small, supported. Anticipate obstacles and identify support.
01What's a small step you could take between now and next time we meet?
02What's likely to get in the way — and how might you handle that?
03When and where would this happen — what does it look like, practically?
04Who could support you with this? What would that support look like?
05What will you do instead of [behaviour] in those high-risk moments?
06How will you know it's working?
07If a setback happens — and they often do — how would you like to handle it?
08Let me check I've got this right — your plan is [summary]. Have I captured it?
