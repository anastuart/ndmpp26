Socratic method steps
01Understand the belief - ask the person to clearly state their belief/argument.
02Sum up the person's argument. Play back what they said to clarify your understanding of their position.
03Ask for evidence - Ask open questions to elicit knowledge and uncover assumptions, misconseptions, insconsistencies, and contradictions
04Challenge their assumptions.
Clarification
Asking questions that help clarify the person's ideas, language, or understanding
01What do you mean when you say [x]?
02Could you explain that further? I want to make sure I'm understanding you correctly.
03Can you give me an example of what that looks like for you?
04How would you explain that concept in simpler terms?
05What is the core thing you're trying to say with that?
06How would you describe that to someone who had never experienced it?
07Is there another way to put that — one that feels even more accurate?

Probing Assumptions
Challenging the assumptions that underlie a person's thinking or conclusions
01What are you assuming in this situation?
02Why do you think that assumption is true?
03Is that always true — or are there times when it doesn't hold?
04What would happen if that assumption weren't true?

Probing Reasons & Evidence
Asking for the reasoning or evidence that supports a person's argument or belief
01Why do you think that's the case?
02What evidence do you have to support that?
03Can you think of a reason why someone might disagree with this?
04Is there any evidence that might point in a different direction?
05How confident are you in that — and what would it take to change your mind?
06Are you basing that on facts, or on how it feels? Can you tell the difference in this case?
07What's the strongest piece of evidence you have for that view?

Exploring Viewpoints & Perspectives
Encouraging consideration of different perspectives or alternative interpretations
01How might someone with a very different viewpoint see this situation?
02What would someone who disagrees with you say in response to that?
03Is there another way of interpreting what happened — one that's equally plausible?
04How do you think [the other person] experienced that situation?
05If your closest friend described this situation, what interpretation might they offer?
06What would a compassionate observer say about what happened — including about you?
07Are there any interpretations you've been actively avoiding? What makes those hard to consider?

Probing Implications & Consequences
Exploring the potential outcomes or implications of a belief, decision, or course of action
01What might happen if you continued to think — or act — this way?
02What are the possible consequences of this idea?
03What might happen if everyone thought this way?
04If that belief is true — what does it mean for how you live your life going forward?
05How does this affect other areas of our discussion?
06What would be different if you let go of this way of thinking?
07What short-term and long-term consequences do you see — even if they're uncomfortable to sit with?

Questioning the Question
Reflecting on the nature and purpose of the question itself — meta-level inquiry
01Why do you think I asked that question?
02What does this question assume about your situation — or about you?
03Is that even the right question to be asking — or is there a more important one underneath it?
04What question are you most avoiding asking yourself right now?
05What would it mean to find an answer to that question — what's at stake?
06What does the way you're framing that question tell you about how you see the situation?

Downward Arrow — Surface to Core
Following a thought downward from surface interpretation to the core belief beneath it
01And if that were true — what would that mean to you?
02What does that say about you as a person — if you really believed it?
03And if that were true — what are you most afraid it means?
04What's the worst thing about that being true — the part that feels most unbearable?
05Does this connect to a belief you've held for a long time — about yourself, others, or the world?
06What rule or belief does this seem to confirm — even if you've never put it into words before?
