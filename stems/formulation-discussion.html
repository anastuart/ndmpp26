Presenting a Formulation

Stems for sharing a formulation collaboratively, setting treatment goals, and managing in-session reassurance-seeking — supporting the client to make sense of their difficulties alongside you.

Collaborative Sense-Making

Presenting a formulation is an invitation to make sense of the client's experience together. Offer your formulation tentatively, use the client's own words, and check understanding throughout. The goal is a shared map of what working together is going to look like.

Formulation · CBT · Collaborative Practice
Opening & Framing
Establishing a collaborative stance from the outset — signalling openness, inviting the client's perspective, and introducing the formulation as something to explore together
01What has brought you in to see me today?
02Over the past few sessions, I've been putting together a picture of what's been going on for you. I'd like to share that with you today and see if it makes sense — and please do tell me if anything doesn't fit.
03I want to share what I've been noticing — but I'm genuinely curious to hear your reaction to it as we go.
04Does any of that resonate with you?
05Tell me if anything feels off or doesn't quite fit your experience.

Presenting Problem
Anchoring the formulation in what the client has shared — reflecting back the presenting concerns and checking your understanding before going further
01I want to start by reflecting back what you've shared with me over these sessions — just to make sure I've understood it right.
02The main things you've been struggling with seem to be [X, Y, Z] — does that sound about right?
03Before I go further, is there anything you'd add or change about that picture?

Predisposing Factors
Exploring what set the stage — early experiences, core beliefs, and temperamental tendencies that created a vulnerability before the current episode began
01Sometimes it helps to think about what might have set the stage for these patterns — the experiences or beliefs that were already there before things got difficult.
02I want to share some thoughts about what might have made you more vulnerable to this — and please tell me if any of it doesn't ring true.
03Does any of that resonate with you?

Precipitating Factors
Identifying what triggered the current episode — the events or changes that activated existing vulnerabilities and brought things to a head
01Were there particular times or situations where things got worse?
02Looking back, was there a moment when things shifted from manageable to overwhelming?
03What do you think set this off, or made things harder when they did?

Perpetuating Factors
The clinical core — explaining what keeps the problem going, validating the client's coping strategies, and connecting the maintenance cycle to the treatment direction
01Here's the tricky part — the very things you do to manage the anxiety actually end up feeding it. Does that make sense when you think about your own experience?
02It makes complete sense you developed these strategies. They work in the short term. It's just that they don't give the worry a chance to settle on its own.
03You actually noticed this yourself — when you [describe the client's own experiment], the anxiety went up and then settled. That's really important data.
04If we drew a cycle of what happens — the trigger, the thought, the feeling, and what you do — what would it look like?
05What we often see with worry like this is that it's not just about the content of the worries — it's about your relationship with uncertainty. Your mind has learned that not knowing something is dangerous, and worry feels like a way of managing that. But the reassurance-seeking and checking actually tell your brain that the uncertainty was worth being afraid of — so the alarm stays on.
06Does that picture feel like it captures what's been happening for you?

Protective Factors
Ensuring strengths are named alongside difficulties — noticing what's already working and what the client brings to the therapeutic work
01I also want to make sure we notice what's working in your favour — the things that are already helping, even if they feel small.
02Even in the middle of all this, I've noticed some real strengths — I want to name those too.
03What do you think has kept things from getting even harder?

Setting Treatment Goals
Transitioning collaboratively from shared understanding to personally meaningful, specific goals — and introducing the treatment rationale
01Now that we've mapped out what's happening, I'd like us to think together about what you'd most like to be different. What would a good outcome from therapy look like for you?
02If the worry was less of a problem, what would you be doing differently?
03What would the people close to you notice had changed?
04What would tell you therapy had been worth it?
05The approach we'll take is about helping you build a different relationship with uncertainty — not eliminating worry entirely, but learning that you can tolerate not knowing, and that the anxiety will pass without you needing to do something to fix it. Those small experiments you've already tried? That's exactly the direction we're heading.
06Where would you most like to start — is there one area where you feel ready to make a change?
07On a scale of zero to ten, how important is it to you to work on [goal]? And how confident do you feel that you could make progress with some support?

Managing In-Session Reassurance-Seeking
Responding therapeutically when clients check in mid-session — turning the behaviour into collaborative data rather than reinforcing it with direct reassurance
01I notice you're checking in with me about how you're doing — that's actually really relevant to what we've been talking about. What do you think is happening there?
02What would happen if you sat with that uncertainty for a moment, rather than checking with me?
03I wonder if we've just seen the pattern live — what do you make of that?
04That question you just asked me — is that a familiar feeling? What's underneath it?
05If I gave you a reassuring answer right now, what do you think would happen to that feeling in the next ten minutes?
