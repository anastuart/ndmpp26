Verbal Risk Assessment

Stems for assessing suicide risk — exploring suicidal thoughts, plans, access to means, prior behaviour, and protective factors to inform clinical decision-making.

Suicidal Thoughts
Exploring the presence, onset, persistence, and controllability of suicidal ideation.
01Does the client have suicidal thoughts?
02When did these begin?
03How persistent are they?
04Is the client able to control the thoughts?
05What is stopping the client from acting on the thoughts?

Presence of a Suicide Plan
Assessing whether the client has a specific plan, including method and frequency of planning thoughts.
01Does the client have a plan?
02Have they specified a method or place?
03How often is the client thinking about their plan?
PlanMethodIntent
Access to Means
Determining whether the client has the means available to carry out a plan, and how lethal those means are.
01Does the client have access to the means (e.g. weapons, medication) to carry out their plan?
02How lethal is the method?
03Does the client have an occupation that may mean they have access to lethal means (e.g. police officer, health worker)?
MeansLethalityAccess
Previous Suicidal Behaviour
Exploring prior suicidal ideation, self-harm history, and parallels with the current situation.
01Has the client had suicidal feelings before?
02Do they have a history of self-harm?
03What were the details and circumstances of previous attempts?
04Are there similarities in the current situation?

Screening Question Hierarchy
A graduated sequence of questions that gently leads to directly asking about suicidal thoughts, feelings, plans, or behaviour.
01"Are you feeling hopeless about the present or future?"
02"Have you ever thought that you'd rather not be here?"
03"Have you ever thought about hurting yourself?"
04"Have you made any plans to take your life?"

Protective Factors
Recognising and promoting factors that reduce suicide risk across personal, work, family, and community domains.
01Adaptive coping skills (Personal)
02Effective problem-solving skills (Personal)
03Self-understanding and sense of competence (Personal)
04Spirituality (Personal)
05Supportive work environment and positive relationships with colleagues (Work)
06Access to employee assistance programs (Work)
07Relationship to family and sense of responsibility (Family)
08Community involvement and access to affordable, supportive services (Community)

Suicide Risk Management
Steps to decide, in consultation with the client, how to manage risk and maintain safety following assessment.
01Develop a safety plan with the client.
02Make referrals to other health professionals or services as appropriate.
03Consider contacting the client's family or support network (with consent).
04Arrange a formal risk assessment if indicated.
